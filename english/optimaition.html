OPTIMIZATION

Web site optimization is the process of using controlled experimentation to improve a website’s ability to drive business goals.

Our Timeline

Let’s dive into the SEO timeline and take a deeper look at what to expect.

SEO Baby Steps

It can be exciting to implement fresh SEO-friendly changes. While can see the new content on your site, observe the optimization in the smaller details, and observe valuable backlinks, it’s going to be a while before you start to see “results.”

The initial process of optimizing business website for SEO can be slow to gain speed. This is especially true if weren’t focusing previously on SEO or adopting a new strategy. The early stages of SEO are all about putting down roots for further growth. Patience is key, and it can be helpful to recognize that visible success may not be seen for several months.

Growing Pains

SEO can be a clunky process at times. Every business is different, and determining what works and what doesn’t sometimes require trial and error, even for seasoned pros. Results should ultimately be much more consistent when working with professional web marketing and SEO experts.

For the first few months, may notice subtle changes, but might not observe anything solid for close to a year. Depending on your business, competing to rank for certain terms can present a more magnificent challenge. Comprehensive keyword research can give you a huge advantage, but growth will still take time.

Google and other search engines are continuously “crawling” the web and adjusting rankings, etc. But this is not an instantaneous process. So while a valuable backlink may be directing an increase of traffic to your site and improving your Internet clout, it may take up to a few weeks for Google to register and catalogue it. Thus, you might notice positive growth with initial hits to your site, but your rankings may not be reflecting the change–yet.

What are “Results?”

We provide clients with detailed monthly reports showing the hard data and numbers. This is what most small businesses are thinking of when they look for SEO results. But success goes beyond these simple numbers, and instead depends heavily on the behavior of web users and potential customers.

Working with an experienced web marketing company can be paramount here, as this gives you the insight needed to examine and re-examine the behavior of users of your site as it relates to SEO. High traffic may seem beneficial, but its value is lost if the many leads, you’re generating aren’t converting into sales. Refining your SEO strategy can zero in to figure out how to angle tactics appropriately. In essence, good SEO brings you the RIGHT kind of traffic, and this is an end goal that can take time.

Adaptation

Google is constantly changing their ranking criteria. The number one search engine is famous for its frequent shifts in algorithms. What this means cannot rest upon your SEO laurels. To remain relevant and hang on to what you’ve gained, it is vital to remain abreast of these changes and adjust SEO strategy accordingly.

Metamorphosis

A good rule of thumb is to assess your SEO strategy after at least 6 months, and preferably closer to a year. It is at this time that the SEO seeds you’ve planted will truly begin to sprout and the results will be your reward.

In the meantime, trust the process, invest your SEO money in a company known to deliver exceptional work, and watch the changes unfold.

WHY OPTIMIZATION IS IMPORTANT...??

Search engine optimization is nowadays more important than ever and it is necessary for every webmaster to understand the true meaning of SEO as well as the potential it creates for every business.

In simpler terms, Search Engine Optimization is a way to improve your website so that it will appear closer to the top positions in the search results of Google, Yahoo, Bing or other search engines. When you perform a search on Google the order by which the returning results are displayed, is based on complex algorithms. These algorithms (also known as search engine ranking algorithms), take a number of factors into account before they decide which web page will be shown in the first place, second place, etc.
